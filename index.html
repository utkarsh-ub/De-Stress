DE-STRESS!

Give your Stress Wings and Let it Fly Away

Has there ever been a more stressful time in medicine? Today’s doctor finds themself contending with the stress of cumbersome EHRs, short staffing, declining reimbursements, and the potential for burnout. The dreaded coronavirus pandemic that has snuffed out over many lives has taken a toll on the mental health of doctors. Doctors have been upset because of fear that something might happen unexpectedly therefore Stress management should be an integral part of the curriculum of doctors so that they can serve humanity efficiently and effectively during the pandemic and in future.

Why we need to Destress?

Stress is our body’s response to pressure.Stress can be a key cause of anxiety and depression and is sometimes linked to self-harm and suicide.too much stress can cause negative effects. It can leave us in a permanent stage of fight or flight, leaving us overwhelmed or unable to cope. can also have serious physical impacts.

  • Stress can increase the risk of heart disease.
  • It can affect our ability to get to sleep and stay asleep.
  • It is linked to muscle pain.
  • It can also damage the immune system.

Effective ways to Destress

Although Stress is inevitable,it can be manageable.Take a break

  • Even short breaks from work or a person you find difficult can help manage the stresses and strains of daily life.
  • Try a few minutes ofmindfulness each day. We know from research that mindfulness is effective in reducing stress and anxiety.
  • Listening to music can have a tremendously relaxing effect on our minds and bodies
  • Setting up a support group of psychiatrists who regularly remain in touch with other physicians can be a useful strategy
  • Focus on positive things to develop a healthier outlook.
